§01

Brief

THE CHALLENGE

The objective was to design a fully functional, 3D-printable rover capable of traversing uneven terrain while adhering to strict budget and weight constraints. The design needed to be modular for easy repair and assembly.

THE SOLUTION

We utilized a rocker-bogie suspension system adapted for 3D printing tolerances. The chassis was optimized using topology optimization to reduce weight without sacrificing structural integrity.

§03

Iterative Design & Prototyping

The development process began with a rough sketch of the rocker-bogie mechanism, which is famous for its ability to traverse obstacles twice the height of the wheel diameter. We moved quickly into SolidWorks to create a parametric model.

Over 4 iterations were printed. The first version suffered from layer delamination at the suspension joints. We adjusted print orientation and increased wall thickness to 4 perimeters, solving the structural issues without adding metal reinforcement.
